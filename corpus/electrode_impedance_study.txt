Electrochemical Impedance Spectroscopy — Hydrogel Electrodes
Experiment ID: EIS-2026-041
Date: February 8, 2026
Operator: D. Arphie

SETUP
- Working electrode: hydrogel disc (CHG-026-003), 12 mm diameter, 2 mm thick
- Counter electrode: Pt mesh
- Reference electrode: Ag/AgCl (3M KCl)
- Electrolyte: phosphate-buffered saline (PBS, pH 7.4)
- Instrument: Gamry Reference 600+
- Frequency range: 100 kHz – 0.01 Hz
- AC amplitude: 10 mV rms
- Temperature: 37°C (body-temperature simulation)

RESULTS
Impedance at 1 kHz: 285 Ω (target < 500 Ω for neural interface viability)
Phase angle at 1 kHz: -22°
Charge transfer resistance (Rct): 148 Ω
Solution resistance (Rs): 42 Ω
Double-layer capacitance (Cdl): 3.8 µF/cm²

COMPARISON WITH LITERATURE
- Standard Pt electrode at 1 kHz: ~1200 Ω (Cogan, 2008)
- PEDOT:PSS coated Pt: ~150 Ω (Cui & Martin, 2003)
- Our hydrogel: 285 Ω — within viable range for chronic neural recording

STABILITY
- Impedance measured daily for 14 days in PBS at 37°C
- Day 1: 285 Ω → Day 7: 310 Ω → Day 14: 342 Ω
- Drift: +20% over 14 days (acceptable for short-term implants)
- No delamination or cracking observed visually

NOTES
- The hydrogel conformally wraps around curved substrates (tested on 5 mm radius rod).
- Mechanical compliance is orders of magnitude better than rigid metal electrodes.
- Sterilisation via ethylene oxide did not significantly affect impedance (+8%).
