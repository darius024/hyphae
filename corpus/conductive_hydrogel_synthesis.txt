Conductive Self-Healing Hydrogel — Synthesis Protocol
Lab: Advanced Materials Group, Imperial College London
PI: Dr. Elena Vasquez
Date: January 2026

OBJECTIVE
Develop a PEDOT:PSS-based hydrogel with conductivity >0.5 S/cm and
autonomous self-healing efficiency >80% at room temperature.

MATERIALS
- PEDOT:PSS (Clevios PH1000, Heraeus)
- Polyvinyl alcohol (PVA), Mw 89,000–98,000
- Borax (sodium tetraborate decahydrate)
- Dimethyl sulfoxide (DMSO), 5 vol% as secondary dopant
- Glycerol, 10 vol% as plasticiser
- Deionised water

PROCEDURE
1. Dissolve PVA (10 wt%) in DI water at 90°C under stirring for 2 h.
2. Cool PVA solution to 25°C.
3. Add PEDOT:PSS dispersion (30 vol% of total) and stir for 30 min.
4. Add DMSO (5 vol%) and glycerol (10 vol%); stir for 15 min.
5. Add borax solution (0.5 M, 3 mL per 20 mL batch) dropwise.
6. Pour into silicone mould and cure at 25°C for 12 h.

RESULTS (Batch CHG-026-003)
- Conductivity: 0.62 S/cm (4-point probe, 25°C)
- Self-healing efficiency: 87% (tensile recovery after 6 h, 25°C)
- Tensile strength: 42 kPa
- Elongation at break: 320%
- Swelling ratio (24 h, DI water): 180%

OBSERVATIONS
- DMSO secondary doping significantly enhanced conductivity (0.62 vs 0.18 S/cm without).
- Borax cross-link density controls healing time vs mechanical strength trade-off.
- Glycerol prevents dehydration over 7-day ambient exposure.
- Colour: deep blue-black, semi-transparent in thin films (<1 mm).

NEXT STEPS
- Test conductivity retention after 100 healing cycles.
- Evaluate biocompatibility via L929 fibroblast viability assay.
- Attempt 3D printing with modified extrusion parameters.
