Battery Cycling Experiment Log
Lab: Materials Science Building, Room 204
PI: Dr. Chen

Experiment ID: BC-2026-017
Date Range: January 15 - February 10, 2026
Objective: Evaluate capacity retention of NMC-811 cathode with novel electrolyte additive FEC-3

Cell Configuration:
- Cathode: LiNi0.8Mn0.1Co0.1O2 (NMC-811), loading 12.5 mg/cm2
- Anode: Graphite, 14.2 mg/cm2
- Electrolyte: 1M LiPF6 in EC:DMC (1:1) + 2% FEC-3
- Separator: Celgard 2500

Cycling Protocol: CC-CV at C/3, voltage window 2.8-4.3V, 25°C

Results Summary:
- Initial capacity: 198.3 mAh/g (cycle 1)
- Capacity at cycle 50: 191.7 mAh/g (96.7% retention)
- Capacity at cycle 100: 185.2 mAh/g (93.4% retention)
- Capacity at cycle 200: 172.8 mAh/g (87.1% retention)
- Coulombic efficiency: >99.5% after cycle 5

Observations:
- FEC-3 additive shows improved capacity retention vs. baseline (82.3% at 200 cycles without additive)
- EIS measurements show reduced charge-transfer resistance at cycle 100 (15.2 ohm vs 23.7 ohm baseline)
- Post-mortem SEM reveals thinner, more uniform CEI layer with FEC-3
- Gas evolution during formation cycles was 30% lower than baseline

Conclusion:
FEC-3 additive at 2% concentration shows promising improvement in NMC-811 cycling stability.
Recommend follow-up experiments at 1%, 3%, and 5% concentrations.
Temperature dependence study at 45°C also recommended.
