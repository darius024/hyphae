Biocompatibility Assessment — Conductive Hydrogel CHG-026-003
Experiment ID: BIO-2026-008
Date: February 12, 2026
Operator: S. Marin

OBJECTIVE
Evaluate cytotoxicity and cell adhesion properties of the PEDOT:PSS/PVA
hydrogel (batch CHG-026-003) for potential use as a chronic neural interface.

CELL LINE
- L929 mouse fibroblasts (ATCC CCL-1)
- Passage number: 14–18
- Culture medium: DMEM + 10% FBS + 1% pen/strep
- Incubation: 37°C, 5% CO2

METHODS
1. Direct contact: hydrogel discs (8 mm) placed on confluent cell monolayers.
2. Extract test: hydrogel incubated in culture medium (72 h, 37°C), extract
   applied to cells at 100%, 50%, 25% dilutions.
3. Cell viability: MTT assay at 24 h, 48 h, and 72 h.
4. Live/dead staining: calcein AM / ethidium homodimer at 72 h.

RESULTS
Direct Contact:
- 24 h viability: 94.2% ± 3.1%
- 48 h viability: 91.8% ± 2.7%
- 72 h viability: 89.5% ± 4.0%
- No visible zone of inhibition around hydrogel disc.

Extract Test (72 h):
- 100% extract: 87.3% ± 3.5% viability
- 50% extract:  93.1% ± 2.8% viability
- 25% extract:  96.7% ± 1.9% viability
- Negative control: 100% ± 2.1%

Live/Dead Staining:
- >90% live cells in direct contact region.
- Cell morphology normal — spread, elongated fibroblast shape.
- No evidence of membrane blistering or nuclear condensation.

CONCLUSION
CHG-026-003 passes ISO 10993-5 cytotoxicity threshold (>70% viability).
The hydrogel is classified as non-cytotoxic for fibroblast culture.
Recommend follow-up with primary cortical neurons and astrocytes for
neural interface application. In vivo subcutaneous implantation study
planned for Q2 2026.
