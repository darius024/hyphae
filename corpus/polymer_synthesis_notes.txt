Polymer Synthesis Notes — Conductive Hydrogel Project
Researcher: Stefi
Last Updated: February 2026

Background:
Developing self-healing conductive hydrogels for wearable biosensors.
Target: conductivity >1 S/cm, self-healing efficiency >85%, biocompatible.

Synthesis Batch SH-042:
- Monomer: Acrylic acid (AA, 2.0M) + PEDOT:PSS (1.2 wt%)
- Crosslinker: N,N'-methylenebisacrylamide (MBA, 0.5 mol%)
- Initiator: Ammonium persulfate (APS, 0.2 mol%)
- Reaction: 70°C, 4 hours under N2

Results:
- Conductivity: 0.87 S/cm (below target)
- Self-healing efficiency: 91% after 6h at room temp
- Tensile strength: 45 kPa
- Swelling ratio: 12.3x in PBS

Batch SH-043 (increased PEDOT:PSS):
- Changed PEDOT:PSS to 1.8 wt%
- Conductivity: 1.23 S/cm (ABOVE TARGET)
- Self-healing efficiency: 78% (below target — crosslink density too high?)
- Tensile strength: 52 kPa
- Swelling ratio: 9.8x in PBS

Key Insight:
Increasing PEDOT:PSS improves conductivity but may interfere with dynamic bond
reformation needed for self-healing. Need to find the sweet spot or introduce
additional dynamic crosslinks (e.g., boronate ester bonds).

Next Steps:
1. Try intermediate PEDOT:PSS concentration (1.5 wt%)
2. Add phenylboronic acid (PBA) as dynamic crosslinker
3. Test biocompatibility with L929 fibroblast cell line
4. Measure impedance at skin-electrode interface
