Literature Review — Self-Healing Conductive Polymers for Bioelectronics
Compiled: January 2026

1. Tee et al. (2012) — Nature Nanotechnology
"An electrically and mechanically self-healing composite with pressure- and flexion-sensitive properties"
- Nickel microparticles in a hydrogen-bonding supramolecular polymer.
- Conductivity: ~0.01 S/cm, healed in ~15 min.
- Limitation: low conductivity, not suitable for neural interfaces.

2. Oh et al. (2016) — Nature
"Intrinsically stretchable and healable semiconducting polymer for organic transistors"
- DPP-based conjugated polymer with dynamic urea bonds.
- Healed at 150°C, 24 h — not practical for biological settings.
- Carrier mobility recovered to 85%.

3. Zhang et al. (2020) — Advanced Materials
"A highly stretchable, self-healing, conductive hydrogel for wearable sensors"
- PAA/PEDOT:PSS hydrogel with Fe³⁺ coordination cross-links.
- Conductivity: 0.12 S/cm, self-healing at RT in 2 h.
- Gauge factor: 3.4 (good for strain sensing).

4. Lu et al. (2022) — Science Advances
"Tough, self-healing, and tissue-adhesive hydrogel electronics"
- PVA/PEDOT:PSS with boronate ester dynamic bonds.
- Conductivity: 0.38 S/cm, self-healing efficiency >90% in 4 h.
- Demonstrated in vivo EMG recording for 3 days.
- Closest prior work to our approach — we improve conductivity to 0.62 S/cm.

5. Wang et al. (2023) — Nature Electronics
"Intrinsically conductive hydrogels for neural interfaces"
- Pure PEDOT:PSS hydrogel (no insulating matrix).
- Conductivity: 47 S/cm — much higher, but brittle and non-self-healing.
- Excellent impedance, limited mechanical robustness.

KEY GAPS IN LITERATURE
- No prior work combines conductivity >0.5 S/cm with autonomous RT self-healing.
- Biocompatibility data is sparse for PEDOT:PSS hydrogels in chronic implant settings.
- 3D-printability of self-healing conductive hydrogels is unexplored.
- Long-term conductivity retention after repeated healing cycles is unknown.
